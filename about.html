About KEDI

The story behind Kano's entrepreneurship revolution

Our History

The Entrepreneurship Development Institute (EDI) 2012 established 26 entrepreneurship institutes across Kano. These centers were designed to tackle unemployment head-on by providing practical skills training. After years of neglect, Governor Abba Kabir Yusuf (Abba Gida Gida) revived the institutes in 2024.

Since revival,the Institutes has trained over 5,870 individuals in diverse fields: poultry, livestock, fisheries, informatics, film production, driving, and more. The institutes has graduated batches with tools, cash, and startup support, directly contributing to the state’s GDP and reducing reliance on white-collar jobs.

Our Mandate

  • Provide free, practical skills training to Kano youth and women.
  • Empower graduates with starter packs and seed capital.
  • Partner with local and international bodies for curriculum development.
  • Foster innovation through ICT and modern farming techniques.
  • Create a sustainable pipeline of entrepreneurs who will build Kano's future.
